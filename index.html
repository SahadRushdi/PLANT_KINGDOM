Plants are the eukaryotes that form the kingdom Plantae; they are predominantly photosynthetic. This means that they obtain their energy from sunlight, using chloroplasts derived from endosymbiosis with cyanobacteria to produce sugars from carbon dioxide and water, using the green pigment chlorophyll.

For more information about Plants visit PLANT KINGDOM


There are mainly 5 types of plant kingdoms. That includes algae, bryophytes, pteridophytes, gymnosperms and angiosperms

Angiosperms

Angiosperms are flowering plants that produce flowers and bear their seeds in fruits.

Gymnosperms

Gymnosperms are cone-bearing plants that bear naked seeds.

Algae

Algae are a members of a group of predominantly aquatic photosynthetic organisms of the kingdom Protista.

Bryophytes

Bryophytes are a group of land plants, sometimes treated as a taxonomic division. Bryophyta consists of the mosses only.

Pteridophytes

Pteridophytes (ferns and lycophytes) are free-sporing vascular plants that have a life cycle with alternating, free-living gametophyte and sporophyte phases that are independent at maturity.
